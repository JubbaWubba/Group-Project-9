Image Adjustment Cheat Sheat

    Saturation -

  • The intensity of a color. High saturation results in more vivid colors while low saturation results in more muted colors.

    Exposure -

  • The overall brightness of an image. Low exposure results in a dark picture and high exposure results in a bright picture.

    Contrast -

  • A degree of difference between tones and colors in a photo. High contrast has less details, but more texture. Low contrast has less textures, but more details.

    Shadows -

  • Shadows: Shadows are the darkest parts of the image. Increasing the shadows would brighten the dark areas of the photo and reveal more the shadow details.

    Highlights -

  • Highlights: Highlights are the brightest parts of the image. Lowering the highlights value will darken the light areas of the photo and reveal more of the highlight detail. Pure whites that already don't have any detail would not be changed with this adjustment.
