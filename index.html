Image Adjustment Cheat Sheet

Saturation -

The intensity of a color. High saturation results in more vivid colors while low saturation results in more muted colors.

Exposure -

The overall brightness of an image. Low exposure results in a dark picture and high exposure results in a bright picture.

Contrast -

A degree of difference between tones and colors in a photo. High contrast has less details, but more texture. Low contrast has less textures, but more details.

Brightness -

Simply how dark or light a picture is. Increasing the brightness will make the image more light and decreasing the brightness will make the picture darker.

Hue -

Hue is color or the characteristic that distinguishes one color from another. Changing the hue will change the colorization of your picture.
