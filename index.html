Image Adjustment Cheat Sheat

  • Saturation: The intensity of a color. High saturation results in more vivid colors while low saturation results in more muted colors.
  • Exposure: The overall brightness of an image. Low exposure results in a dark picture and high exposure results in a bright picture.
  • Constrast: A degree of difference between tones and colors in a photo. High contrast has less details, but more texture. Low contrast has less textures, but more details.
  • Brightness: Simply how dark or light a picture is. Changing the brightness will affect all the pixels equally.
  • Hue: The color within your image.
